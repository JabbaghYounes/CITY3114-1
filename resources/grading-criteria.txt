Introduction and Conclusions (10%)
Does the introduction presents the problem and contain evidence to justify its importance? Having presented all of the material, does the report reach clear conclusions and make suitable suggestions? Do those conclusions relate to the material/evidence provided in the main body, or are they generic and not specific to the particular report?
Background (30%)
Also, does the report contain background information that covers the main concepts of the topic area?
Analysis and Discussion (40%)
Evidence of consideration and understanding of the issue, going beyond the mere presentation of information from other sources to reveal the rationale behind the final recommendation on the basis of the background case already presented. Those opinions should be well-justified and based on evidence.
Presentation and Structure (10%)
Is the material integrated and easy to follow, or does it feel like several segments bolted together in an arbitrary manner? Is the text accessible to a general audience, or does it contain technical terms without explanation?  Is the formatting of the report consistent, or do different elements appear to have originated from different authors? Has the report made good use of figures, tables, etc. to support the main text? 
References and Supporting Evidence (10%)
Does the report cite appropriate, authoritative literature to support its claims?  Do references include a suitable level of bibliographic detail to enable the source to be located (e.g. Harvard style referencing)?  Is relevant supporting material provided in appendices?
Harvard or IEEE style references include in text.
